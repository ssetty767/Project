Bowling

Bowling is an incredibly important part of the game because it's either how you restrict the opposition to a lower score, or defend a score your batting team put on. There's two types of bowling, primarily. Fast bowling and spin bowling.

Fast Bowling

Speeds can go up to 150 kilometers per hour
Spin Bowling

Speeds up to ~120 kilomters per hour

As you can see, fast bowling is about running up and bowling the ball at a high speed. In fast bowling, you can change a couple of factors. You can change the pace (bowl slightly slower using your fingers or knuckles), or change the length (where the ball bounces, if it does). In fast bowling, the ball can also swing, causing it to move in the air and confuse batsmen in the air.

With spin bowling, it's about making the ball bounce in a certain direction to confuse the bastmen straight up. You can change a couple of factors here as well, mainly around the direction the ball spins in. Most spin bowlers have a standard approach (e.g. right arm off spin, which means that the ball will pitch and then bounce left). However you can vary it, by spinning it the other way, or keep it straight and put no spin. A spin bowler can also edit the length and speeds, but the main variations come in the spin of the ball mainly.
