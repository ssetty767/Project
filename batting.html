Batting

Batting is one of the key aspects of cricket. In order to set a high target for the opposition or chase down a tough total, you must score runs. Batters can use a variety of shots in order to get runs. Here are some of them (click on a button to see a different shot):


This shot is played by swinging the bat in a vertical arc through the line of the ball, hitting the ball usually diagonally in front of the batting player along the ground.



Batters go out to bat in pairs. While one person is at the "striker's" end facing the ball, the other batsmen is at the non-striker's end. When the striker hits the ball, both batters must touch the other side of the batting pitch in order to score one run. They have to do this before the ball is thrown to hit the wickets and get them out. Batsmen can cross as many times as they want in the span of time it takes for the fielding team to collect the ball. See an example here of batsmen running to score a run after hitting the ball:

Batsmen can also score 4 or 6 runs by hitting the boundary. If the ball goes over the boundary, it is 6 runs, but if it stays on the ground and touches the boundary rope, it's 4 runs. This is a great way to score runs quickly. If you would like to see batting in action, you could head to the Performances section.
